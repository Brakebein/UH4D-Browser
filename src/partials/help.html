Help

Search

As this is a prototype application, there is a manageable amount of images stored in our database. Hence, if no search term is entered in the search bar, all images will be listed as results.

To narrow the results, enter keywords into the search bar. Currently, the application will look for

  • Title
  • Author/photographer
  • Date
  • Owner
  • Tags
  • Capture number

The results are displayed in the column to the right in ascending or descending order (by title, author, or date).

Navigation – 3D viewport

Navigation in 3D space is primarily done pressing different mouse buttons. But there is also a navigation bar to assist the navigation.

Mouse gestures

There are multiple mouse gestures available:

  • Hold: Rotate view, Click: Select item, Double click: Select and zoom/focus item
  • Hold: Pan view
  • Scroll: Zoom view
  • Click: Select item and open context menu
  • Click: Select/deselect multiple items

Navigation bar

In addition to the mouse gestures, there is also the possibility to choose a navigation mode and navigate with the left mouse button pressed.

  • Fit the whole scene into the viewport
  • Zoom to and focus selected items
  • Standard navigation mode, see "Mouse gestures"
  • Rotate/orbit view with left mouse button pressed
  • Pan view with left mouse button pressed
  • Zoom view with left mouse button pressed
  • Choose between different rendering modes of the 3D objects

Images

All search results are listed in the column to the right. All images that have been spatially oriented are also displayed within the 3D viewport. If the image is spatially oriented or not, is indicated by the icon.

The spatially oriented images (3D images) can be faded and scaled by the slider in the top-right of the viewport.

To open the detailed view of the image with further metadata and an image viewer for closer inspection, click on the result list entry or right-click on the 3D image to open the context menu and choose Open details... .

You are able to take up the position of the photographer. To do so:

  • Double click on the 3D image
  • Right-click on the 3D image and choose Focus
  • Select the image and hit the button in the navigation bar
  • Click on the item at the result list entry

When focusing the image/taking up the position, the image will be isolated. So, other images won't occlude the geometry/3D models. To exit the isolation mode, press the Exit isolation button at the lower-left of the viewport, or just move on.

Selecting an image is done by simply clicking on the 3D image. Multiple images can be selected by holding Ctrl while clicking. All the selected images are listed within the selection box.

3D objects – Buildings

The 3D objects represent the buildings of the city. They are used to provide a spatial context to the photographs, so the user can comprehend the photographer's situation.

Additionally, the 3D objects can be used to narrow the search results. Right-click on a 3D object to open the context menu and choose

  • either Filter include to search only for those images that depict this building
  • or Filter exclude to search only for those images that do not depict this building.

To remove the filter, right-click again on the the building to open the context menu and choose Remove filter, or press the button of the filter entry within the panel at bottom-left corner of the viewport.

Collection – Memorizing images

Images can be memorized. All memorized images can be found within the Collection tab. To add/remove an image to/from the collection, you can

  • Hit the star icon ( ) within the search results list entry
  • Right-click within the viewport on an image to open the context menu and choose Add to collection / Remove from collection
  • Press the responsible button within the detail view

Reorder the images of the collection by drag and drop an entry to the desired position.

Compare images

Two images can be compared and inspected to each other. To enter the comparison view, select two images or have at least two images in your collection. Press the Compare button that connects two selection/collection entries.

The comparison view has two image viewers side by side. You are now able to zoom and pan each image individually. Additionally, there is the possibility to overlay a grid to estimate some distances comparatively.

Analysis tools

There a few prototypical visualization methods that are supposed to support art and architectural historians in their research.

Each method can be toggled within the panel at the bottom-right corner of the viewport. The visualizations will be visible on ground level of the 3D scene. Check the Overlay checkbox to overlie all 3D objects with the visualization. The Radius slider adjusts in which radius images are considered at each sample point.

Heat map – Frequency distribution

Visualization of hotspots of spatially oriented images.

Vector field

Visualization of image orientations (glyph-based).

Moving particles

Visualization of image orientations (particle-based).

Spatialize images [only editable mode]

Some images are not yet spatially oriented. You can help to contribute and manually orientate an image.

Open the detailed view of an image you want to orientate and hit the Spatialize button. The 3D viewport will be overlayed with the image. Now, you have to adjust the view to match the image. Use the opacity slider to fade between viewport and image and the field of view (FOV) slider to adjust the FOV of the virtual camera.

To set up the view more precisely, there are also some vernier adjustment buttons. You can pan and rotate the virtual camera by tiny amounts according to the precision slider. Hit Save when you are done.
